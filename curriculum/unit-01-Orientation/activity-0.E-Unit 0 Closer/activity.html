Part 1 — confidence check

How confident do you feel about each topic?

Rate yourself from 1 (never heard of it) to 5 (could explain it to someone else). This is not graded. Be honest — overrating yourself helps no one, including you.

Your teacher sees this

Your confidence ratings are aggregated across the whole class into a heatmap. If 80% of students rate "AP pseudocode" at 1 or 2, your teacher knows to spend more time on it before the exam. Your honest rating makes the class better for everyone.

Part 2 — reflection

Two questions

Question 1
What's one thing you learned in Unit 0 that genuinely surprised you — something you didn't expect to encounter in a CS class?
Question 2
What's one thing from Unit 0 you're still confused about? Be specific — "I don't get it" isn't useful. "I don't understand why AP pseudocode uses ← instead of =" is.
Part 3 — goal card

Three goals for the year

Write one goal in each category. These will be saved and returned to you at the end of the course — the last week of school, before the AP exam results arrive. Future you will read what you wrote today.

What makes a good goal

Specific beats vague. "Understand how recommendation algorithms actually work" is better than "learn CS." "Build something I'm proud enough to show someone" is better than "do well in class." Write something you actually care about.
